The terrain

Now every sampled next-token position becomes a point. Ground position is a lossy UMAP of context; height is the selected pair's surprisal gap in bits.

Read the height with the scale at right: 1 bit = 2×, 10 bits = about 1000×, and the labeled Go-struct peak is roughly 25 bits = 33 million×.

Tap or click peaks to see both models' bit bars. A peak means disagreement on the observed continuation; it does not mean one model is correct.

The map of models

The model map compares model priors. Dot distance means "these two models predict differently"; axes are not canonical. Pick two dots and the terrain re-forms for that pair.

The same-data pairs flatten: gpt2 to gpt2-xl and pythia-1.4b to pythia-410m. Data-different pairs erupt into mountains.

Scale still nudges the map: gpt2-xl sits a little closer to other families than gpt2. But data family dominates the shape.

What this means

Base models are not neutral averages. Each is a prior shaped by what its training corpus made familiar. Alignment later rides on top of this terrain.

  • Curated sample, not natural density. This is a domain-balanced, tail-oversampled sample. Faithful mode shows where priors differ, not how often text naturally lands there.
  • UMAP is lossy. The x-y projection is chosen for readability; the axes are not canonical.
  • Surprisal gap is not correctness. This is |bitsA - bitsB| on the observed continuation, not symmetric JSD and not a score of which model is better.
  • Flattening is elevation, not tail shape. Same-data pairs make shorter mountains, but the p99/median tail shape still overlaps; gpt2 to gpt2-xl flattens less than the Pythia pair.
  • Scale is second-order. Bigger models nudge the terrain, but data differences dominate.
