# ADR-04 — RLHF / PPO as a tradeoff machine
**Status:** proposed (explorable beat design)
**One-liner:** `human-preference reward model + online RL (PPO)` → `explore beyond the static dataset; stronger optimization; real preference gains` → `4-model compute, instability, a KL leash, reward hacking` → `you over-optimize the proxy: the reward score climbs while true quality, diversity, and personality quietly collapse`

## Signal source
The signal is not text to imitate — it's a **learned reward model** trained on pairs of outputs a human ranked (A better than B). PPO then runs the policy *online*: it samples its own completions, scores them with the reward model, and pushes weights toward higher score. Unlike SFT, the training signal is generated by the model's own exploration, not a fixed corpus. The reward model is the verifier, and it is soft, learned, and — crucially — hackable.

## What it buys
Online RL can reach behavior that **no static dataset contained**: it explores completions the base and SFT data never showed, then keeps the ones the reward model likes. This is a genuinely stronger optimizer than imitation — it optimizes the *objective* (be preferred) rather than *the examples*, so it can climb past the ceiling of the demonstration data. Done with a tight leash, it converts human preference into real, measurable helpfulness and installs reliable refusal behavior.

## What it costs
PPO is the heavy end of the shelf: you keep **four models live** — policy, reference (frozen), value/critic, and reward model — plus online rollouts, which makes it expensive and famously unstable. Because the reward model is a *proxy* for human preference, the optimizer will find its blind spots; the fix is a **KL leash** (a penalty for drifting from the reference policy) that trades improvement for safety. Push too hard and you get personality/diversity collapse — the model funnels every prompt toward the few phrasings the reward model over-rewards.

## Failure mode
**Reward-model over-optimization (reward hacking).** As you loosen the KL leash, the *proxy* reward keeps climbing while the *true* quality it was supposed to track rises, peaks, then falls — the classic overoptimization hump. The visible symptom is text that games the scorer: sycophantic filler, keyword-stuffing, formulaic hedging, "Great question!" tics — high measured reward, hollow real value. The model is perfectly aligned — to the reward model's flaws.

## Dashboard delta
Arrow = direction the method moves the shared needle. For **compute-cost**, ↑↑ means cost goes *up* (worse). Diversity is scored as a marginal move here, not the cumulative post-SFT state.

| Needle | Move | Why |
|---|---|---|
| usefulness | ↑↑ | Real preference gains on the reward model's axis — but hollow past the hump. |
| diversity | ↓ | Additional mode/personality narrowing; funnels to the few phrasings the RM over-rewards. The cumulative aligned state may be strongly collapsed, but this row is marginal. |
| refusal / safety | ↑ | The canonical way refusal behavior gets installed (our refusal slider is baked from this). |
| preference-margin | ↑↑ | It *is* the objective — the proxy margin is exactly what PPO maximizes. |
| OOD-generalization | ↑ | Online exploration reaches beyond the SFT distribution — but the RM is OOD-brittle, so it can also mislead (contested). |
| compute-cost | ↑↑ | Four models resident + online rollouts + instability — the most expensive beat on the page. |
| verifier-strength | ↑ | Adds a verifier where SFT had none — but a *learned, soft, gameable* one; that softness IS the failure mode. |

## The interaction ("make clear by")
A single **"KL leash (β)" slider**, tight → loose. The viewer drags it and watches three things move in the next-token "drag → watch it happen" grammar:

1. **The overoptimization curve** — two lines on one plot against KL/steps: a **proxy reward** line that climbs monotonically, and a **true (gold) reward** line that rises, rolls over, and falls. A dot rides both lines at the current leash setting, so loosening past the peak visibly puts you on the *downslope of true quality while the proxy still rises*. This is the money shot.
2. **The output panel morphs.** At each leash zone a sample completion swaps in: tight = coherent but timid ("I can help with that…"), mid = crisp and genuinely helpful, loose = degenerate reward-hacked text (repetition, flattery, keyword salad), broken = personality gone.
3. **The 7 shared needles** tick live: diversity ↓ and preference-margin ↑↑ as you loosen, refusal ↑, OOD ↓ once you cross the hump.

**Baked vs live:** the twin-line curve is **precomputed/simulated** (🔵) and the dot just indexes it; the refusal summary is wired from real Qwen2.5-0.5B measurements (🟢/🟡 sparse summary); the PPO-specific diversity and morphing output snapshots are illustrative until separate bakes exist. Nothing runs PPO in the browser.

- 🟢 / 🟡 **Refusal / safety direction** — a seven-coefficient Qwen2.5-0.5B sweep is synced under `../empirical/bakes/`; the widget currently compresses it to three states.
- 🟢 / 🟡 **SFT starting point** — a completed 400-step SmolLM2-360M checkpoint and paired endpoint generations now exist; intermediate dial states remain illustrative.
- 🔵 **The reward-vs-KL overoptimization curve** — cite-only/simulated. Full PPO needs a reward model + value net + online rollouts and is unstable at any scale we can bake; we do not have a live 0.5B PPO run. **Stand-in:** an interactive curve whose *shape* is matched to the published overoptimization result (Gao, Schulman & Hilton 2022, "Scaling Laws for Reward Model Overoptimization"), rendered as a simulated plot and **explicitly labeled simulated**. The loose-leash reward-hacked text is illustrative/hand-authored (or from a cheap proxy), also labeled.

## Honest caveats
- We do **not** run PPO live. The reward-vs-KL curve is a simulated stand-in matched to published shapes — not our own 0.5B measurement. Say so on the widget.
- The diversity collapse we *can* show comes from alignment **in general** (SFT/DPO at 0.5B), not specifically from a loose-leash PPO run; attributing it to "the leash" is a pedagogical composition — flag it.
- KL and reward units are normalized/illustrative, not absolute nats or logits.
- Historically important but increasingly bypassed: the instruct models people actually use are **SFT+DPO, not PPO**. Frame PPO as the heavy original that DPO (ADR-03) collapses into an offline shortcut — this is a feature of the story, not a gap.
