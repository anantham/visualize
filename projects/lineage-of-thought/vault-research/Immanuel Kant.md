# Immanuel Kant

German philosopher whose “Copernican revolution” in epistemology limited knowledge to phenomena while preserving room for faith in the noumenal realm; his ethics of duty and autonomy, aesthetics of the sublime, and political philosophy of perpetual peace shaped German Idealism, liberalism, and modern moral theory.

## Influenced by
- [[David Hume]] — skepticism about causation awakened him from dogmatic slumber
- [[Jean-Jacques Rousseau]] — emphasis on freedom and moral autonomy

## Disagreed with
- [[David Hume]] :: Is causation merely habitual or a priori? — a priori; the category of causality is a necessary condition of experience imposed by the mind

## Cruxes
- [[What are the limits of human knowledge?]] — knowledge is limited to phenomena structured by a priori forms and categories; noumena are thinkable but unknowable
- [[What is the foundation of morality?]] — the categorical imperative: act only according to maxims that can be willed as universal law; treat humanity always as an end, never merely as a means

## Idea travel
- 1781 — Königsberg: publishes Critique of Pure Reason (first edition)
- 1785 — 1788 — Königsberg: Groundwork and Critique of Practical Reason establish moral philosophy
