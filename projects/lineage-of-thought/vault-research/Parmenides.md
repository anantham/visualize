# Parmenides

Parmenides of Elea, active in the early 5th century BCE, wrote a philosophical poem distinguishing the Way of Truth (unchanging Being) from the Way of Opinion (illusory world of change and plurality). His rigorous monism and denial of change influenced Plato's theory of Forms and the entire Western metaphysical tradition, prompting responses from Heraclitus, Empedocles, and atomists.

## Influenced by
- [[Xenophanes]] — critique of anthropomorphic religion and emphasis on unity
- [[Pythagoras]] — southern Italian philosophical culture

## Disagreed with
- [[Heraclitus]] :: Is change real? — change and becoming are impossible; only static Being exists  _(✓2)_
- [[Pythagoras]] :: Is reality mathematical? — Being is prior to plurality and number

## Cruxes
- [[Is change real?]] — no; true reality is eternal, unchanging Being; plurality and motion are deceptive
- [[Can non-being be thought?]] — no; what is not cannot be thought or spoken
