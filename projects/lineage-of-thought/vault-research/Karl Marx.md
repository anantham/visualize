# Karl Marx

German philosopher, economist, and revolutionary whose historical materialism and critique of political economy analyzed capitalism as a historically specific mode of production doomed by its internal contradictions; with Engels he founded modern communism and inspired 20th-century revolutions and social movements worldwide.

## Influenced by
- [[G. W. F. Hegel]] — dialectical method (inverted from idealism to materialism)
- [[Ludwig Feuerbach]] — critique of religion and alienation (transformed into critique of economic alienation)

## Cruxes
- [[What drives historical change?]] — contradictions between productive forces and relations of production; class struggle is the motor of history
- [[What is the nature of alienation under capitalism?]] — workers are alienated from their labor, product, species-being, and fellow humans because they do not control the means of production

## Idea travel
- 1848 — Europe: Communist Manifesto published amid revolutions
- 1917 — Russia: Bolshevik Revolution applies (and transforms) Marxist theory
- 1949 — China and beyond: inspires anti-colonial and socialist revolutions across the global South; remains central to critical theory and political economy
