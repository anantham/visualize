# Rene Descartes

French philosopher, mathematician, and scientist often called the father of modern philosophy; his method of radical doubt and emphasis on clear and distinct ideas inaugurated rationalism, analytic geometry, and a mechanistic view of the physical world while seeking to ground knowledge on an unshakeable foundation.

## Cruxes
- [[Is knowledge innate or acquired?]] — innate ideas (God, self, mathematical truths) are present in the mind; sensory experience requires rational validation
- [[What is the relationship between mind and body?]] — substance dualism; mind is thinking, unextended substance; body is extended, non-thinking substance

## Idea travel
- 1637 — Leiden: publishes Discourse on the Method (anonymously)
- 1641 — Paris/Netherlands: Meditations dedicated to the Sorbonne
- 1649 — Stockholm: moves to court of Queen Christina; dies the following year
