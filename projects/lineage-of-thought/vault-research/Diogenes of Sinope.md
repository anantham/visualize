# Diogenes of Sinope

Diogenes of Sinope, exiled from Sinope, lived ascetically in Athens and Corinth, famously dwelling in a tub and searching for an honest man with a lantern. He radicalized Socratic self-examination into Cynic practice: virtue through extreme simplicity, autarkeia (self-sufficiency), and parrhesia (frank speech), rejecting wealth, fame, and social norms as obstacles to happiness. He influenced Zeno and Stoicism.

## Influenced by
- [[Antisthenes]] — Socratic austerity and virtue
- [[Socrates]] — ethical independence and public questioning

## Develops
- [[Socrates]] — radicalizes the examined life into bodily, social, and political nonconformity

## Disagreed with
- [[Plato]] :: What is the good life? — virtue requires lived simplicity, not metaphysical theory
- [[Aristotle]] :: Is politics natural? — conventional citizenship is secondary to cosmopolitan freedom

## Cruxes
- [[What is the good life?]] — radical self-sufficiency and living in accordance with nature, free of convention
- [[Is philosophy a way of life?]] — yes; philosophy must be embodied in practice
- [[Is social status real?]] — no; status is a corrupt convention
