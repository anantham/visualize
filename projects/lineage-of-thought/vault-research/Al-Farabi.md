# Al-Farabi

“Second Teacher” (after Aristotle) who synthesized Platonic, Aristotelian, and Neoplatonic thought into a systematic Islamic political philosophy; his works on logic, music, psychology, and the ideal city profoundly shaped later Islamic and Jewish thinkers and transmitted Greek science to the medieval West.

## Influenced by
- [[Aristotle]] — logic, ethics, and metaphysics (via Syriac and Arabic translations)
- [[Plato]] — Republic and political philosophy (harmonized with Aristotle)

## Cruxes
- [[What is the best political order?]] — a virtuous city ruled by the philosopher-imam who unites theoretical wisdom with practical governance
- [[How do religion and philosophy relate?]] — religion is a symbolic imitation of philosophical truth suited to the multitude

## Idea travel
- 900 — Baghdad: studies with Syriac Christian logicians and produces major works on logic and music
- 950 — Aleppo/Damascus: completes Virtuous City; influences Avicenna and later falasifa
- 1100 — Islamic Spain and Jewish thought: transmitted via Ibn Bajja and Maimonides
- 1200 — Latin West: translations (Gerard of Cremona) shape Scholastic political thought as Alpharabius
