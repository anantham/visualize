# Averroes

“Andalusian commentator” whose detailed Aristotelian commentaries (especially the Long Commentary on the De Anima) shaped Latin Averroism and Jewish philosophy; he defended philosophy against al-Ghazali’s critique, arguing for the harmony of faith and reason and the unity of the intellect.

## Influenced by
- [[Aristotle]] — exhaustive commentaries that became authoritative in the West
- [[Al-Farabi]] — Islamic Peripatetic tradition (critiqued and refined)

## Disagreed with
- [[Al-Ghazali]] :: Can philosophy be reconciled with religion? — yes; apparent contradictions arise from misreading scripture allegorically or taking philosophical conclusions literally; the elite should study philosophy

## Cruxes
- [[Is the world eternal or created in time?]] — eternal in the sense that it has no temporal beginning (matter and celestial motion are perpetual)
- [[Is the intellect individual or one for all humans?]] — the active (agent) intellect is numerically one, shared by all; personal immortality is through the acquired intellect

## Idea travel
- 1200 — Latin Europe (Toledo translations): Long Commentary on De Anima sparks Latin Averroism at Paris and Bologna
- 1300 — Jewish and Christian thought: influences Maimonides’ successors, Siger of Brabant, and Dante; later condemned but never fully suppressed
