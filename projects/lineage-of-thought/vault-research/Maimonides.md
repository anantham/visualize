# Maimonides

Preeminent medieval Jewish philosopher, codifier of Jewish law (Mishneh Torah), and physician to the Ayyubid court; his Guide for the Perplexed reconciled biblical revelation with Aristotelian metaphysics and influenced both Jewish and Christian Scholastic thought while provoking controversy among traditionalists.

## Influenced by
- [[Aristotle]] — metaphysics, logic, and psychology

## Cruxes
- [[Can God’s attributes be known positively?]] — no; only negative attributes (what God is not) are possible without compromising divine simplicity
- [[What is the purpose of the commandments?]] — ethical, political, and intellectual perfection; many have rational explanations

## Idea travel
- 1148 — flees Almohad persecution from Spain to North Africa then Egypt
- 1200 — Jewish communities: Guide sparks Maimonidean controversies in Provence and Spain
- 1300 — Latin Christendom: translations influence Aquinas and Scholastics; remains central to Jewish philosophy
