# Jean-Jacques Rousseau

Genevan philosopher whose writings on education (Emile), politics (Social Contract), and autobiography (Confessions) ignited Romanticism and influenced the French Revolution; he argued that civilization corrupts innate human goodness and that true freedom lies in obedience to the general will one has helped to form.

## Influenced by
- [[John Locke]] — empiricism and some educational ideas (critiqued)

## Disagreed with
- [[Thomas Hobbes]] :: Is human nature inherently selfish or good? — inherently good; corrupted by society and inequality, not by original sin or natural egoism

## Cruxes
- [[Is human nature good?]] — yes; “man is born free, and everywhere he is in chains”; chains are of society’s making
- [[What legitimizes the state?]] — the general will; sovereignty resides in the people as a whole, not in a monarch or parliament

## Idea travel
- 1762 — Amsterdam/Paris: publishes Social Contract and Emile (both banned)
- 1789 — French Revolution: Social Contract inspires Jacobins and Declaration of the Rights of Man
