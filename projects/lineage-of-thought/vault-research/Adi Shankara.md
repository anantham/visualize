# Adi Shankara

Systematized and propagated Advaita Vedanta across India through commentaries, debates, and the founding of four major mathas; his hagiographies later portrayed him as a digvijaya (conqueror of the directions) who unified Hindu philosophical traditions under non-dualism.

## Influenced by
- [[Gaudapada]] — non-dual interpretation of the Mandukya Upanishad and the doctrine of maya
- [[Govinda Bhagavatpada]] — his direct teacher who transmitted the Advaita lineage

## Cruxes
- [[Is the self identical with ultimate reality?]] — yes; Atman is Brahman, the sole existent; all else is apparent
- [[Is the world real or illusory?]] — ultimately illusory (maya); only Brahman is sat (real)

## Idea travel
- 700 — Kalady/Sringeri: composes major commentaries and establishes the first Advaita matha
- 800 — India: debates rival schools and spreads non-dual Vedanta through digvijaya tours
- 1300 — Vijayanagara Empire: Vidyaranya’s hagiographies and imperial patronage revive and institutionalize his legacy
- 1900 — Global: Swami Vivekananda and neo-Vedanta carry Advaita to the West and modern Hindu reform movements
