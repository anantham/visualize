# John Locke

English philosopher and physician whose empiricism and political liberalism shaped the Enlightenment, the American and French Revolutions, and modern conceptions of rights and government; his rejection of innate ideas and defense of limited government and toleration remain foundational to liberal democracy.

## Influenced by
- [[René Descartes]] — rationalist method and clear ideas (critiqued)

## Disagreed with
- [[René Descartes]] :: Are there innate ideas? — no; the mind is tabula rasa; all knowledge derives from experience

## Cruxes
- [[Is knowledge innate or acquired?]] — acquired through sensation and reflection; no innate principles or ideas
- [[What legitimizes political authority?]] — consent of the governed in a social contract to protect natural rights

## Idea travel
- 1689 — England (after Glorious Revolution): publishes Essay and Two Treatises (written earlier in exile)
