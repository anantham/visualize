# Augustine of Hippo

Bishop, theologian, and philosopher whose Confessions pioneered introspective autobiography and whose City of God provided a Christian philosophy of history; he synthesized Neoplatonism with Pauline Christianity, shaping Western theology on grace, original sin, predestination, and the nature of the will for over a millennium.

## Influenced by
- [[Plato]] — metaphysics of intelligible reality and ascent of the soul
- [[Cicero]] — style and early philosophical formation

## Disagreed with
- [[Pelagius]] :: Is grace necessary for salvation or can humans achieve it by free will alone? — grace is absolutely necessary; the will is weakened by original sin and cannot choose the good without divine aid

## Cruxes
- [[Is human nature good?]] — originally created good, but fallen through Adam’s sin; concupiscence remains even in the baptized
- [[What is the relationship between faith and reason?]] — faith seeks understanding; reason can prepare for or confirm faith but cannot replace grace

## Idea travel
- 386 — Milan: conversion under Ambrose’s influence
- 397 — Hippo: writes Confessions
- 410 — Rome sacked: begins City of God as response
- 430 — Hippo: dies during Vandal siege; works copied and preserved in monasteries across Europe, becoming foundational for medieval theology
