# Zhu Xi

Preeminent Neo-Confucian synthesizer who edited and commented on the Four Books, making them the core of the imperial examination system from 1313 to 1905; he established academies, promoted “investigation of things,” and codified a rationalist metaphysics that dominated East Asian thought for centuries.

## Influenced by
- [[Cheng Hao]] — the Cheng brothers’ li-qi metaphysics and moral cultivation
- [[Mencius]] — innate goodness of human nature and the four beginnings

## Disagreed with
- [[Wang Yangming]] :: How is sagehood achieved? — through disciplined study and investigation of principles, not sudden innate knowing alone

## Cruxes
- [[Is human nature good?]] — yes; originally good, but requires cultivation to manifest fully
- [[Are knowledge and action unified?]] — knowledge must precede and guide action; investigation of things is essential

## Idea travel
- 1313 — Yuan dynasty: his Four Books commentaries become official examination curriculum
- 1400 — Ming/Qing China: Cheng-Zhu orthodoxy institutionalized in state ideology and education
- 1600 — Korea/Japan/Vietnam: Zhu Xi studies (Jujahak, Shushigaku) shape East Asian Confucian elites
