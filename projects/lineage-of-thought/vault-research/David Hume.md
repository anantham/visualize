# David Hume

Scottish Enlightenment philosopher whose radical empiricism and skepticism about causation, induction, the self, and miracles challenged rationalist metaphysics and natural theology; his moral philosophy grounded ethics in sentiment and utility, profoundly influencing Kant, utilitarianism, and analytic philosophy.

## Influenced by
- [[John Locke]] — empiricist inheritance (critiqued and radicalized)

## Cruxes
- [[Is causation a necessary connection or constant conjunction?]] — constant conjunction plus psychological habit; no necessary connection in objects or demonstrable by reason
- [[Does the self exist as a substance?]] — no; the self is a bundle or collection of perceptions in perpetual flux

## Idea travel
- 1739 — 1740 — London: publishes Treatise (anonymously, little initial success)
- 1748 — London: Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding reaches wider audience
