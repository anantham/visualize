# B. R. Ambedkar

Jurist, economist, and social revolutionary who chaired the Drafting Committee of the Indian Constitution; born into an untouchable Mahar family, he led mass conversions to Buddhism in 1956, burned the Manusmriti, and fought lifelong for the annihilation of caste through law, education, and political organization.

## Influenced by
- [[John Dewey]] — pragmatism, democracy as a way of life, and progressive education (studied at Columbia)

## Disagreed with
- [[Mahatma Gandhi]] :: How should caste be addressed in the freedom struggle? — separate electorates and legal safeguards vs. moral persuasion and varna reform; caste must be annihilated, not reformed

## Cruxes
- [[Is caste hierarchy justified?]] — no; it is a system of graded inequality that must be destroyed root and branch
- [[Can religion be a force for social justice?]] — yes, if reinterpreted as Navayana Buddhism centered on equality and rationality

## Idea travel
- 1932 — Poona: signs Poona Pact after fast against separate electorates
- 1947 — 1950 — New Delhi: drafts Indian Constitution with fundamental rights and reservations
- 1956 — Nagpur: leads mass conversion to Buddhism; posthumous publication of The Buddha and His Dhamma inspires global Dalit Buddhist movement
