# Kabir

Weaver-poet and mystic of the Sant tradition whose dohas and songs, preserved in the Guru Granth Sahib and Kabir Bijak, critique religious hypocrisy in both Hinduism and Islam while advocating a direct, casteless path to the divine through inner devotion and moral conduct.

## Influenced by
- [[Ramananda]] — his guru in the Ramanandi Bhakti lineage

## Cruxes
- [[Is caste hierarchy justified?]] — no; all humans are equal before the formless divine
- [[Is God personal or impersonal?]] — both; nirguna Absolute yet experienced through loving devotion

## Idea travel
- 1400 — Varanasi: composes and sings dohas criticizing religious orthodoxy
- 1500 — North India: oral transmission spreads among weavers, artisans, and lower castes
- 1600 — Punjab: verses included in Guru Granth Sahib, shaping Sikh tradition
- 1900 — Global: Tagore’s English translations introduce Kabir’s syncretic mysticism to the modern world
