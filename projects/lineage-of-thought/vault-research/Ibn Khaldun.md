# Ibn Khaldun

Tunisian historian, economist, and statesman whose Muqaddimah founded sociology, historiography, and economics as empirical sciences; he analyzed the dynamics of nomadic vs. sedentary societies, the role of religion in state formation, and the inevitable decay of dynasties through loss of asabiyyah.

## Cruxes
- [[What drives the rise and fall of civilizations?]] — asabiyyah (social cohesion) strengthens new dynasties then weakens with luxury and urbanization, leading to decline
- [[Can history be studied scientifically?]] — yes; through observation of recurring patterns in economics, demography, and social psychology

- 1400 — Ottoman and later Arab world: Muqaddimah studied and copied
