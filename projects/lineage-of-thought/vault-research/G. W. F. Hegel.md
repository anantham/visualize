# G. W. F. Hegel

German Idealist whose dialectical method and vision of history as the march of Geist (spirit/mind) toward self-realization dominated 19th-century European thought; his Phenomenology, Logic, and political philosophy influenced Marx, Kierkegaard, existentialism, and contemporary continental philosophy.

## Influenced by
- [[Immanuel Kant]] — critical philosophy and transcendental turn (radicalized)
- [[Johann Gottlieb Fichte]] — earlier Idealists

## Disagreed with
- [[Immanuel Kant]] :: Are things-in-themselves unknowable? — no; the dialectic overcomes the Kantian dualism; reason can know the Absolute because the Absolute is reason coming to self-consciousness

## Cruxes
- [[What is the nature of reality?]] — the Absolute is not substance but subject; it realizes itself through historical dialectical process
- [[What is the end of history?]] — the full self-consciousness of freedom in the rational state and philosophical comprehension of the whole

## Idea travel
- 1807 — Jena: publishes Phenomenology of Spirit amid Napoleonic wars
- 1818 — 1831 — Berlin: delivers famous lectures on history, aesthetics, religion, and philosophy of right
