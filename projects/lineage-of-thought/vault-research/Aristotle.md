# Aristotle

Born in Stagira, Aristotle studied at Plato's Academy for twenty years before founding the Lyceum in Athens. He developed systematic logic, biology, ethics, politics, and metaphysics grounded in observation of particulars. Rejecting transcendent Forms, he held that form and matter are united in substances; the highest human good is eudaimonia achieved through intellectual and moral virtue in a political community.

## Influenced by
- [[Plato]] — his teacher; teleology, ethics, and classification (while transforming them)  _(✓2)_
- [[Socrates]] — ethical inquiry and definition
- [[Parmenides]] :: Is change real? — the challenge of explaining change without contradiction

## Develops
- [[Plato]] — preserves the search for form and explanation while relocating form inside substances

## Disagreed with
- [[Plato]] :: Are universals real? — Forms are immanent in particulars, not separate transcendent entities; universals exist only in things  _(✓2)_
- [[Parmenides]] :: Is change real? — change is intelligible as the actualization of potential

## Cruxes
- [[Are universals real?]] — yes, but immanent in concrete substances, not transcendent
- [[Is change real?]] — yes; change is the movement from potentiality to actuality
- [[What is the good life?]] — flourishing through rational virtue
