# Paulo Freire

Brazilian educator and philosopher whose Pedagogy of the Oppressed became a foundational text of critical pedagogy; exiled after the 1964 coup, he developed literacy methods for adults that linked reading the word with reading the world, influencing popular education, liberation theology, and social movements across Latin America, Africa, and beyond.

## Influenced by
- [[Karl Marx]] — analysis of oppression and structural inequality

## Cruxes
- [[What is the purpose of education?]] — humanization and liberation; to develop critical consciousness and the capacity to transform oppressive realities
- [[What is the relationship between teacher and student?]] — dialogical; teacher-student and student-teacher learn together in a horizontal relationship

- 1970 — Chile/USA/Switzerland: writes Pedagogy of the Oppressed; teaches and consults globally
