# Al-Ghazali

“Proof of Islam” and renewer (mujaddid) who experienced a spiritual crisis, left his prestigious Baghdad professorship, and spent years in Sufi seclusion; his Incoherence of the Philosophers critiqued Avicennian metaphysics while his Revival integrated law, theology, and Sufism into a comprehensive spiritual path that dominated Sunni Islam for centuries.

## Influenced by
- [[Avicenna]] — early philosophical training (later critiqued)

## Disagreed with
- [[Avicenna]] :: Can philosophy demonstrate the truths of religion? — no; philosophers contradict themselves on causality, God’s knowledge, and the afterlife; occasionalism better preserves divine omnipotence

## Cruxes
- [[Can reason alone establish religious truths?]] — no; demonstrative philosophy reaches limits; Sufi unveiling (kashf) provides certainty
- [[Is the world governed by natural causality or divine will?]] — divine will; apparent causes are habitual, not necessary

## Idea travel
- 1095 — Baghdad: spiritual crisis and departure from Nizamiyya madrasa
- 1095 — 1106 — Damascus/Jerusalem/Mecca: Sufi retreat and pilgrimage
- 1106 — Tus: returns and teaches in his zawiya; writes Revival
- 1200 — Islamic world and beyond: works translated into Latin and Hebrew; influences Maimonides, Aquinas, and later mystics
