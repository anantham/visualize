# bell hooks

Black feminist scholar, cultural critic, and educator (born Gloria Jean Watkins) whose accessible, poetic prose and intersectional analysis transformed feminist theory, critical pedagogy, and cultural studies; she insisted that theory must be liberatory, that education can be a practice of freedom, and that love is a revolutionary force.

## Influenced by
- [[Paulo Freire]] — critical pedagogy and problem-posing education (engaged and critiqued from a Black feminist perspective)

## Cruxes
- [[What is the relationship between education and liberation?]] — education as the practice of freedom; teaching to transgress domination and nurture critical awareness
- [[What role does love play in social justice?]] — love is not sentimental but an ethic of care, accountability, and transformation that sustains movements and heals trauma

## Idea travel
- 1981 — USA: Ain't I a Woman? establishes her as major Black feminist voice
