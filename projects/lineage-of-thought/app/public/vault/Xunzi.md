# Xunzi

Xunzi (Xun Kuang), teacher of Han Feizi and Li Si, offered a realist Confucian alternative to Mencius. He argued human nature (xing) tends toward disorder and self-interest; morality and social harmony are artificial achievements produced by ritual, education, and institutional design modeled on ancient sage-kings. His emphasis on li (ritual) as civilizing force and empirical observation influenced Legalism and later ritual studies.

## Influenced by
- [[Confucius]] — develops ritual and self-cultivation, but with realist anthropology  _(✓2)_
- [[Mencius]] — Confucian moral psychology as the position to revise
- [[Mozi]] — concern with standards, order, and social utility

## Develops
- [[Confucius]] — makes ritual an artificial technology for transforming human nature

## Disagreed with
- [[Mencius]] :: Is human nature good? — no; originally bad or neutral-chaotic; requires external shaping by ritual and authority  _(✓2)_
- [[Laozi]] :: Should rulers act forcefully? — cultivated institutions, not natural spontaneity, create order
- [[Zhuangzi]] :: Can language state ultimate truth? — names and distinctions can be rectified for social order

## Cruxes
- [[Is human nature good?]] — no; selfish and disorderly; goodness is the product of deliberate cultivation and ritual
- [[Is ritual central to order?]] — yes; ritual transforms desire into civilized conduct
- [[Should society be engineered by standards?]] — yes; institutions and names must be deliberately ordered
