# Wang Yangming

Ming dynasty statesman, general, and philosopher who developed the School of Mind as a dynamic alternative to Cheng-Zhu rationalism; while suppressing rebellions he formulated the unity of knowledge and action, arguing that moral insight is innate and realized in practice rather than bookish investigation.

## Influenced by
- [[Lu Jiuyuan]] — earlier School of Mind emphasizing the mind as the source of principle

## Disagreed with
- [[Zhu Xi]] :: How is knowledge acquired and verified? — not through external investigation of things first, but through the mind’s innate knowing manifested in action

## Cruxes
- [[Is knowledge prior to action or unified with it?]] — knowledge and action are one; true knowing is acting
- [[Is moral knowledge innate?]] — yes; liangzhi (innate knowing) is present in all people and needs only to be recovered

## Idea travel
- 1600 — Late Ming/Qing: Yangming school influences Taizhou radicals and popular ethics
