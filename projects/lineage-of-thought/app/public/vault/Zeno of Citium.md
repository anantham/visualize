# Zeno of Citium

Zeno of Citium, shipwrecked in Athens around 312 BCE, studied under Cynic Crates and founded Stoicism in the Stoa Poikile. He synthesized Socratic ethics, Cynic asceticism, and Heraclitean physics into a system where virtue alone ensures happiness, the cosmos is rationally ordered by divine logos (fire), and one must accept fate with equanimity. His school dominated Hellenistic and Roman philosophy.

## Disagreed with
- [[Epicurus]] :: What is the good life? — virtue alone (apatheia), not pleasure or tranquility through desire-limitation  _(✓2)_

## Cruxes
- [[What is the good life?]] — life according to nature and reason
- [[Are passions good guides?]] — no; passions are mistaken judgments
- [[Is the cosmos providential?]] — yes; the cosmos is rationally ordered by Logos
- [[What is the highest good?]] — virtue in accordance with nature and reason; emotions from erroneous judgments
