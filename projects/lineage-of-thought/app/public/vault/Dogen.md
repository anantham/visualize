# Dogen

Founder of the Soto school of Zen in Japan; after training under Chinese Caodong master Rujing, he returned to establish Eihei-ji monastery and wrote the monumental Shobogenzo, articulating a rigorous, non-dual vision of zazen as the direct expression of buddha-nature in everyday activity.

## Cruxes
- [[What is the relationship between practice and enlightenment?]] — they are identical; sitting is enlightenment
- [[Is buddha-nature inherent in all beings?]] — yes; it is the impermanent, dynamic suchness of everything, including mountains and rivers

## Idea travel
- 1223 — China (Mount Tiantong): receives transmission from Rujing and masters shikantaza
- 1227 — Japan (Kyoto area): returns and begins teaching; later moves to Echizen
- 1244 — Eihei-ji founded: establishes strict Soto monastic code and writes Shobogenzo
