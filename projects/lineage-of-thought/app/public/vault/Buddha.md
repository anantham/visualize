# Buddha

Siddhartha Gautama, the historical Buddha, renounced princely life, practiced extreme asceticism, then discovered the Middle Way. After enlightenment under the Bodhi tree, he taught for 45 years across northern India: the Four Noble Truths diagnose suffering and prescribe the Eightfold Path. Central insight: no permanent self (anatta); all phenomena arise dependently. His sangha and dharma spread Buddhism across Asia.

## Influenced by
- [[Alara Kalama]] — meditative training
- [[Uddaka Ramaputta]] — formless meditative attainment
- [[Mahavira]] — shared ascetic and renunciant milieu

## Develops
- [[Sramana movement]] — reworks renunciation around the middle way and dependent arising

## Disagreed with
- [[Mahavira]] :: Is the self real? — Middle Way rejects both extreme austerity and Vedic sacrifice; anatta vs. eternal jiva  _(✓2)_
- [[Makkhali Gosala]] :: Is moral effort effective? — intentional action matters for liberation

## Cruxes
- [[Is the self real?]] — no; anatta (no permanent, independent self); suffering from clinging to illusion of self
- [[What causes suffering?]] — craving and ignorance
- [[Can liberation be achieved in life?]] — yes; nirvana is possible through the path
