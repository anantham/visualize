# Mahavira

Mahavira (Vardhamana), the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism, renounced royal life at 30 and achieved kevala (omniscience) after 12 years of austerity. He taught a rigorous path of non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, chastity, and non-possession; every soul (jiva) is eternal and potentially divine, bound by karma that must be burned off through ascetic practice. His community split into Svetambara and Digambara sects.

## Influenced by

## Disagreed with
- [[Buddha]] :: Is the self real? — the soul is real and must be liberated from karma
- [[Makkhali Gosala]] :: Is moral effort effective? — liberation depends on disciplined action, not fatalism

## Cruxes
- [[Is the self real?]] — yes; eternal jiva distinct from body and karma
- [[Does nonviolence have absolute priority?]] — yes; ahimsa is the supreme discipline
- [[Can karma be burned away?]] — yes; austerity and restraint purify the soul
