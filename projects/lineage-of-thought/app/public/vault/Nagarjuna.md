# Nagarjuna

Nagarjuna, founder of Madhyamaka ("Middle Way") school, used dialectical reasoning (prasanga) to demonstrate the emptiness of all dharmas. His Mulamadhyamakakarika deconstructs essentialist views, showing that concepts like causation, self, and nirvana have no independent reality yet function conventionally. This profoundly shaped Mahayana Buddhism, influencing Yogacara, Tibetan, and East Asian traditions.

## Influenced by
- [[Buddha]] — Prajnaparamita sutras and core teachings on emptiness and dependent origination  _(✓2)_
- [[Prajnaparamita Sutras]] — emptiness and the bodhisattva path
- [[Abhidharma]] — analytic Buddhist categories he critiques

## Develops
- [[Buddha]] — makes dependent arising and emptiness philosophically explicit

## Disagreed with
- [[Sarvastivada]] :: Do things have intrinsic nature? — dharmas do not possess svabhava
- [[Nyaya]] :: Can reason establish ultimate foundations? — all foundational theses are empty

## Revives
- [[Buddha]] — retrieves the middle way across several centuries of Buddhist scholastic development

## Cruxes
- [[Is the self real?]] — no; even dharmas lack inherent existence; emptiness is the ultimate
- [[Do things have intrinsic nature?]] — no; emptiness is dependent arising
- [[Can language state ultimate truth?]] — only indirectly; ultimate truth is reached through deconstructing views
