# Lev Vygotsky

Soviet psychologist whose cultural-historical theory emphasized the social origins of mind and the mediating role of language and tools; although suppressed in the USSR for decades, his ideas on the zone of proximal development, scaffolding, and the unity of thought and language became foundational to educational psychology, developmental theory, and sociocultural approaches worldwide.

## Influenced by
- [[Karl Marx]] — historical and social determination of consciousness

## Disagreed with
- [[Jean Piaget]] :: Is cognitive development primarily individual maturation or socially mediated? — socially mediated; higher functions appear first on the interpsychological plane, then internalized

## Cruxes
- [[What is the relationship between thought and language?]] — dialectical unity; language is a tool that transforms thought; private speech is a stage in internalization
- [[How does learning relate to development?]] — learning leads development; the ZPD shows what a child can achieve with assistance today that becomes independent tomorrow

## Idea travel
- 1934 — dies of tuberculosis; Thought and Language published posthumously
