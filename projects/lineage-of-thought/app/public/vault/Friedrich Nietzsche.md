# Friedrich Nietzsche

German philosopher and cultural critic whose proclamation “God is dead,” critique of slave morality, and call for the Übermensch and revaluation of values shattered 19th-century certainties; his perspectivism, emphasis on life-affirmation, and genealogical method influenced existentialism, postmodernism, psychology, and literary theory.

## Influenced by
- [[Arthur Schopenhauer]] — will and pessimism (critiqued and transformed)

## Disagreed with
- [[Plato]] :: What is the value of traditional morality? — it is slave morality born of ressentiment; it negates life, strength, and nobility in favor of pity, equality, and the herd

## Cruxes
- [[Is there objective truth or morality?]] — no; all knowledge and values are perspectives rooted in drives and power relations; truth is a mobile army of metaphors
- [[How should one live after the death of God?]] — create new values; affirm life through eternal recurrence; become who you are
