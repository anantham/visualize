# Ramanuja

Theologian and reformer of Sri Vaishnavism who wrote extensive commentaries on the Brahma Sutras, Bhagavad Gita, and Upanishads; he reorganized major temples, advocated inclusion of lower castes in devotional life, and established the qualified non-dual (Vishishtadvaita) school as a major alternative to Advaita.

## Influenced by
- [[Yamunacharya]] — his predecessor whose works and temple reforms he continued

## Disagreed with
- [[Adi Shankara]] :: Is ultimate reality qualified or unqualified? — Brahman is saguna (with qualities) and personal (Vishnu); souls and matter are real modifications, not illusory

## Cruxes
- [[Is the self identical with ultimate reality?]] — no; souls are eternally distinct yet dependent modes of Brahman
- [[Is bhakti or jnana the path to liberation?]] — bhakti (devotion and surrender) is primary and accessible to all

## Idea travel
- 1077 — Sriperumbudur/Srirangam: begins temple reforms and philosophical teaching
- 1100 — South India: institutionalizes Sri Vaishnavism through temple networks and inclusion of non-Brahmins
- 1900 — Global: Vishishtadvaita influences modern Hindu thought and interfaith dialogue
