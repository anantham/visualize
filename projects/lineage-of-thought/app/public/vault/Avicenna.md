# Avicenna

Persian polymath whose Canon of Medicine became the standard medical textbook in both Islamic and European universities until the 17th century; his philosophical encyclopedia The Book of Healing synthesized Aristotelian logic, physics, and metaphysics with Neoplatonic elements, distinguishing essence from existence and profoundly influencing Scholasticism.

## Influenced by
- [[Al-Farabi]] — political philosophy and emanation metaphysics
- [[Aristotle]] — logic, natural philosophy, and metaphysics (via Arabic translations)

## Cruxes
- [[Is there a distinction between essence and existence?]] — yes; in all contingent beings existence is added to essence; only God is necessary existence
- [[What is the nature of the soul?]] — rational soul is immaterial and survives death; intellect actualizes potential through abstraction

## Idea travel
- 1000 — Bukhara/Hamadan: composes Canon and Healing while serving various courts
- 1100 — Islamic world: Avicennism becomes dominant Peripatetic school
- 1150 — Latin Europe: translations (Toledo) make Canon standard in medical faculties
- 1200 — Paris/Oxford: influences Albertus Magnus and Thomas Aquinas; condemned in 1210/1215 but later rehabilitated
