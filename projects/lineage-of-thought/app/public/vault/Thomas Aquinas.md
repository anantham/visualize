# Thomas Aquinas

Dominican friar and Doctor of the Church who synthesized Aristotelian philosophy (recovered via Islamic and Jewish translations) with Christian theology; his Summa Theologica remains the most systematic exposition of Catholic doctrine, balancing faith and reason, nature and grace.

## Influenced by
- [[Aristotle]] — metaphysics, ethics, psychology, and natural philosophy (via Arabic-Latin translations)
- [[Augustine of Hippo]] — theology of grace and the will (critiqued and refined)
- [[Avicenna]] — Islamic and Jewish Aristotelian commentaries

## Cruxes
- [[Are universals real?]] — yes, moderate realism: universals exist in things (in re) and in the mind (post rem); they are not separate Platonic Forms
- [[Is human nature good?]] — yes; created good, wounded by sin but capable of natural and supernatural virtues
