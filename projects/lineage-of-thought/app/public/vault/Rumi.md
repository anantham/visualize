# Rumi

Persian Sufi mystic, poet, and founder of the Mevlevi (whirling dervish) order; his encounter with the wandering dervish Shams Tabrizi transformed him from a sober jurist into the ecstatic poet of divine love whose Masnavi and ghazals remain among the most widely read and translated works in the Islamic world and beyond.

## Cruxes
- [[What is the path to God?]] — love and annihilation of the self (fana); the dance of the dervish mirrors the cosmic dance of creation
- [[Are religious forms ultimate or relative?]] — relative; all paths and scriptures are fingers pointing to the same moon of divine reality

## Idea travel
- 1244 — Konya: transformative meeting with Shams Tabrizi
- 1273 — death in Konya: tomb becomes major pilgrimage site
- 1900 — Global: translations (especially by Coleman Barks) make Rumi the best-selling poet in the United States; Mevlevi order revives in Turkey and diaspora
